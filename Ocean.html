Great White Sharks

Great Whites play an important role in keeping the populations of certain species under control

They help in keeping diversity and stability for ocean species populations under control.

Surprisingly, the main predator for sharks are HUMANS. Fishermen gather sharks and cut off their fin for their monetary value, and they are used to make 'shark fin soup.'

Great White Sharks are normally found in coastal waters along The Northeast and West Coast, as well as South Africa, Japan, Chile, Oceania, and the Meditteranean.
